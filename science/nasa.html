Operation Dominic

Operation Dominic was a series of 105 nuclear test explosions conducted by the United States in 1962. These tests took place primarily over the Pacific Ocean and were part of an effort to understand the effects of nuclear weapons in different environments. Operation Fishbowl was a subset of Operation Dominic, focusing specifically on high-altitude nuclear tests. The tests were conducted to study the impact of nuclear explosions in the upper atmosphere, and included several tests with various yields. These tests were crucial in understanding how nuclear detonations in space could affect satellite communications, radar, and other technological systems.

The operation, named "Dominic" after Saint Dominic, whose name means "of the Lord," presents an interesting point. Considering that Operation Fishbowl was a part of Operation Dominic, these operations could collectively be interpreted as "Fishbowl of the Lord." This choice of name may be viewed as another example of symbolic or potentially esoteric significance.

Wernher von Braun

Wernher von Braun was a pioneering aerospace engineer who played a key role in developing rocket technology and space exploration. Initially working on the V-2 rocket in Germany, he later became a central figure at NASA, leading the development of the Saturn V rocket that powered the Apollo missions to the Moon. His contributions were crucial in advancing the United States' space program.

As one of NASA's founding figures, von Braun's leadership and vision were instrumental in the success of America's early space missions. His work laid the foundation for human space exploration, making him a significant figure in NASA's history.

Von Braun was also deeply religious, particularly in his later years. He believed in the harmony between science and religion, viewing space exploration as a way to understand God's creation. His faith was reflected on his gravestone, which is inscribed with Psalm 19:1: "The heavens declare the glory of God; the skies proclaim the work of his hands."

Dr. Carol Rosin, a former aerospace executive and close associate of Wernher von Braun, testified that in his final years, von Braun warned of a sequence of fabricated threats that could be used to justify the weaponization of space. According to Rosin, von Braun predicted that these threats would begin with concerns about communism, followed by terrorism, then rogue nations, and finally, extraterrestrial threats.
